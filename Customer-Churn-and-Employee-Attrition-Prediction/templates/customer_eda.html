Customer Churn EDA

Introduction

Customer churn prediction is the process of identifying customers who are likely to stop using a product or service in the near future. This technique is widely used in various industries, including telecommunications, banking, and e-commerce, to forecast customer behavior and develop retention strategies. Churn prediction models can be built using machine learning algorithms and historical customer data to predict the likelihood of customer churn.

Jointplots

Jointplots are a type of data visualization technique used to display the relationship between two variables in a dataset. They combine two different plots into one, showing a scatter plot of the data along with a probability density function or a histogram for each variable. This helps to identify patterns, correlations, and outliers in the data, making it easier to interpret and draw insights from the data. Jointplots are commonly used in data analysis and statistical modeling to understand the relationship between two variables and make data-driven decisions.

Pairplot Visualisation

Seaborn's pairplot is a type of visualization that allows for the creation of a grid of plots, where each variable in a dataset is plotted against every other variable. This creates a visual representation of the pairwise relationships between the variables, making it easier to identify patterns and correlations in the data.

Each plot in the pairplot displays a scatterplot of the two variables and a histogram of each variable's distribution. Additionally, diagonal plots show a kernel density estimate of the distribution of each variable. Pairplots can also be customized with various options, including adding regression lines, changing the color scheme, and displaying categorical variables using different symbols or colors. Overall, pairplot is a powerful tool for exploratory data analysis, as it allows for quick and comprehensive visual inspection of relationships between variables in a dataset.
